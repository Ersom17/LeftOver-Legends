
## Food Storage and Handling Practices

| **Food Category** | **United States** | **Switzerland** | **Food‑Waste Insight** |
|------------------|------------------|-----------------|------------------------|
| **Eggs** | Washed → protective cuticle removed; **must be refrigerated** at all times | Unwashed → natural cuticle intact; **stored at room temperature up to ~21 days**, then refrigerated | Misunderstanding leads to unnecessary disposal when practices are mixed |
| **Milk** | Mostly **pasteurized, refrigerated milk**; short shelf life (7–14 days) | **UHT milk common**; shelf‑stable at room temp until opened | UHT milk reduces spoilage and over‑refrigeration |
| **Bread** | Often **refrigerated**, plastic‑wrapped; preservatives common | **Never refrigerated**; stored at room temp in paper/cloth; bought fresh | Refrigeration accelerates staling of European bread |
| **Cheese (hard/aged)** | Refrigerated; mold often treated as spoilage | Stored cool; **small mold trimmed off**; breathable wrapping | Hard cheeses are low‑moisture and resilient |
| **Cheese (soft)** | Refrigerated; discard at mold | Refrigerated; stricter handling | Aligns across cultures |
| **Cured meats (salami, prosciutto)** | Typically refrigerated; sliced products common | **Whole dry‑cured meats may be stored cool**, refrigerated after slicing | Whole cuts last longer than pre‑sliced |
| **Fresh meat & poultry** | Refrigerated immediately | Refrigerated immediately | No meaningful difference |
| **Potatoes** | Often mistakenly refrigerated | Stored **cool, dark, ventilated** (not fridge) | Refrigeration increases spoilage & sweetness |
| **Onions** | Sometimes refrigerated or stored with potatoes | Stored **separately**, cool & ventilated | Ethylene gas accelerates rot |
| **Garlic** | Pantry or fridge | Pantry | Stable when dry |
| **Tomatoes** | Frequently refrigerated | **Stored at room temperature** | Cold damages flavor & texture |
| **Fruits (general)** | Refrigerated by default | Selective refrigeration | Over‑refrigeration increases waste |
| **Jam / preserves** | Usually **refrigerated after opening** | Often refrigerated, but pantry storage accepted for high‑sugar jams | Sugar + acidity already inhibit microbes |
| **Leftovers** | Large portions; freeze encouraged | Smaller portions; quicker reuse | Portion size strongly affects waste |
| **Shopping habits** | Large, infrequent grocery trips | Smaller, more frequent shopping | Shopping frequency drives storage behavior |

## Food Date Labels & Safety Meaning

| **Topic** | **United States** | **Switzerland** |
|----------|------------------|-----------------|
| **Best By / Best If Used By** | Quality indicator, **not safety** | Quality indicator |
| **Use By** | Often quality‑based (except infant formula) | **Strict safety date**; illegal to sell past |
| **Selling past date** | Allowed for most foods | Allowed past _Best Before_, **not past Use By** |
| **Consumer behavior** | Dates heavily influence discard | Sensory checks emphasized |
| **Impact on waste** | High waste due to date confusion | Lower waste for shelf‑stable foods |
