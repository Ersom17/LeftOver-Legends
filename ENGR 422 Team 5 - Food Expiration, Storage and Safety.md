# Food Expiration, Storage and Safety Guide

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1542838132-92c53300491e

Understanding how long food lasts and how to store it properly helps keep you safe, save money, and reduce food waste. This guide explains common date labels, how to spot spoiled food, and best practices for storing, freezing, and handling food.

---

## Understanding Food Date Labels

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1586201375761-83865001e17b

Food date labels can be confusing. In most cases, they describe food quality rather than food safety. Knowing the difference helps prevent unnecessary waste.

### Common Date Terms Explained

- **Best By / Best Before**
  - Indicates peak quality and flavor
  - Often safe to eat after this date if stored properly

- **Use By**
  - Usually found on perishable foods
  - Suggests the last date of best quality
  - Some items may be unsafe after this date

- **Sell By**
  - Used by stores for inventory management
  - Food is usually safe for days or weeks after this date

- **Expiration Date**
  - Less common
  - May indicate safety for specific products such as infant formula

Tip: For most foods, your senses matter more than the printed date.

## How to Tell If Food Has Gone Bad

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1604908554168-9b6e4c2c8f63

Use sight, smell, and texture to determine if food is still safe to eat.

### Fruits and Vegetables

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1590080876265-6d87c06a1c8b

- Mold with fuzzy green, white, or black spots
- Slimy or mushy texture
- Strong or fermented smell
- Excessive leaking or browning

Some produce may still be usable if only slightly soft or wrinkled.

---

### Dairy Products

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1580910051074-7c7d9b7cdb6f

- Sour smell
- Curdling or separation in milk
- Mold growth on soft cheeses
- Unusual flavor

Hard cheeses may be safe if small mold spots are cut away.

---

### Meat, Poultry and Seafood

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1603052875614-1409bfeae6c2

- Sticky or slimy surface
- Rotten or sulfur-like odor
- Gray, green, or dull coloring

Do not taste meat or seafood to test freshness.

---

### Eggs

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1517959105821-eaf2591984c7

- Strong or foul smell after cracking the egg
- Float test: eggs that float may be old and should be inspected carefully

---

## Leaving Food Out: Time Matters

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1605379399642-870262d3d051

Bacteria grow quickly at room temperature, especially on cooked and perishable foods.

### Safe Time Limits

- 2 hours maximum at room temperature
- 1 hour if temperatures are above 90°F (32°C)
- Refrigerate leftovers promptly in shallow containers

Avoid leaving cooked foods out overnight.

---

## Best Food Storage Practices

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1586201375761-83865001e17b

Proper storage helps extend food life and reduce the risk of contamination.

### Refrigerator Best Practices

- Keep temperature at or below 40°F (4°C)
- Store raw meat on the bottom shelf
- Use airtight containers for leftovers
- Label leftovers with preparation dates

### Pantry Storage Tips

- Store food in a cool, dry place
- Keep dry goods in sealed containers
- Use First In, First Out rotation when possible

---

## Freezing Food Safely

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1586864388093-7b7d7d37ae74

Freezing food slows spoilage and helps reduce waste when done correctly.

### Best Freezing Practices

- Freeze food before it spoils
- Use freezer-safe, airtight packaging
- Remove excess air from containers or bags
- Label food with the date it was frozen

### Typical Freezer Storage Times

- Meat: 6 to 12 months  
- Cooked meals: 2 to 3 months  
- Fruits and vegetables: 8 to 12 months  
- Bread: 2 to 3 months  

Frozen food remains safe indefinitely, but quality decreases over time.

## Reducing Food Waste

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1593113598332-cd288d649433

Reducing food waste saves money and lowers environmental impact.

### Key Food Waste Facts

- About one third of all food produced globally is wasted
- Food waste is a significant contributor to greenhouse gas emissions
- Households are one of the largest sources of food waste

---

## SDG 12.3: Responsible Consumption and Production

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1501004318641-b39e6451bec6

The United Nations Sustainable Development Goal 12.3 aims to:

> Halve global food waste per capita by 2030

### Ways Individuals Can Support This Goal

- Learn how to read food labels
- Store food properly
- Freeze food before it spoils
- Use leftovers creatively
- Buy only what is needed

Small actions can make a meaningful difference.

---

## Final Takeaway

https://images.unsplash.com/photo-1514512364185-4c2b0b052efb

Food dates are helpful quality indicators but do not always mean food is unsafe. By storing food correctly, trusting your senses, and acting early, you can reduce waste, save money, and support a more sustainable food system.
